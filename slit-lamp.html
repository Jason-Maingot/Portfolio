Slit Lamp Simulator

Overview

Slit Lamp Simulator is a virtual version of a slit lamp, a critical diagnostic tool used by ophthalmologists, to give medical students hands-on practice in a virtual environment. Because physical slit lamps are often in use and hard to access, this simulation lets students train more often and with more flexibility.

During this development cycle, the goal was to create a strong minimum viable product (MVP) to demonstrate the concept’s potential and secure additional funding. The simulation was developed in Unity for the Meta Quest headset at the University of Miami’s Virtual Experience and Simulation Lab (VESL)—an XR innovation hub for students and faculty-led projects.

The project was led by Dr. Giselle Ricur, an ophthalmologist and digital health specialist at the Bascom Palmer Eye Institute. I joined the project as a Developer, and was responsible for building and polishing features according to the Primary Investigator (PI)’s vision and user feedback.

Key Features

  • Integrated a 3D model of a slit lamp with accurate proportions and controls
  • Designed an immersive examination room environment
  • Added a virtual patient model for realistic interaction
  • Enabled customization of room lighting, table, and chair height to accommodate users of varying statures
  • Developed a functional examination light with color filters and adjustable size/intensity
  • Simulated real-world lamp movement and adjustment for accurate hands-on practice
  • Built a virtual camera system with zoom capabilities, plus a video screen for viewing
  • Implemented hover states and highlighting for all interactive elements to improve usability and onboarding

Iterations

We held regular working sessions with Dr. Ricur, who frequently tested the simulation and provided detailed feedback from her clinical experience. Her input led to iterative refinements including:

  • Adjusting object positions to match real-world ergonomics
  • Calibrating the zoom function to better reflect actual slit lamp optics
  • Improving UI/UX based on feedback from VR beginners and users unfamiliar with ophthalmology tools

The MVP was showcased at eMerge 2024, where attendees provided valuable feedback—especially for those new to VR or slit lamp usage. Their insights helped ensure the simulator remains intuitive and accessible for first-time users, such as medical students.

Future Development

With the MVP now complete, the next team plans to:

  • Integrate educational modules, including simulated eye conditions
  • Conduct formal testing with real ophthalmology students to evaluate learning outcomes and further refine the simulator

Takeaways

The execution of this project pushed me to think critically, work collaboratively, and bring an ambitious idea to life through hands-on problem-solving and innovation. Some skills I pushed throught the process include:

  • VR Development: Gained a deeper understanding of developing immersive VR experiences through the Unity XR Interaction Toolkit.
  • Team Collaboration: Collaborated closely another developer, working in a shared code repository with emphasis on clean documentation, tight version control, and consistent communication.
  • User-Centered Design: Designed with a focus on clarity, comfort, and usability thanks to PI and user feedback—especially for non-technical users.
