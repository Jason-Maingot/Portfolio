Return to Manufacturer

Overview

Return to Manufacturer was built as a result of Game/Music Jam 2021—a 48-hour event where developers and musicians collaborate to create a fully functional game paired with an original soundtrack.

I served as lead developer and programmer, implementing the game's core systems and contributing heavily to gameplay and systems design. Our four-person team also included specialists in level design, art, and overall game design.

The event’s prompt, "Return," directly shaped our theme and mechanics. We designed a gameplay loop centered around retrieving and delivering packages, with a unique forward-and-backward dash mechanic and a boomerang as the primary weapon—both literal twists on the idea of return.

Takeaways

The biggest challenge was the strict 48-hour time limit—plus starting from scratch with a team of mostly strangers. After outlining our skills and assigning roles, I was chosen to lead development based on my programming and engine experience. We picked Unity for its flexibility and my familiarity with the platform.

We quickly scoped ideas, set realistic goals, and stayed in constant communication through a nonstop live working session, keeping decision-making and feedback fast and efficient. Throughout the project, I worked closely with the designer to adjust and scale features, making sure we hit the deadline without sacrificing quality. Key learnings include:

  • Managing time under extreme pressure and prioritizing essential tasks
  • Communicating technical limitations clearly to non-technical teammates
  • Rapid learning and problem-solving, including quickly mastering Unity’s animation system
  • Building hands-on experience with game and level design, from contributing ideas to giving actionable feedback
